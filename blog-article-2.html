5 Ways to Maximize Your Truck's Fuel Efficiency

Published on May 28, 2026 • 5 min read

Fuel is undeniably the single largest recurring expense for any independent truck owner. Even a 5% improvement in fuel economy can result in thousands of dollars in savings annually. Here are 5 proven ways to squeeze more mileage out of every drop of diesel.

1. Reduce Engine Idling

An idling heavy-duty truck engine burns about 0.8 gallons of fuel per hour. While drivers need to stay comfortable, investing in Auxiliary Power Units (APUs) or cab heaters can drastically reduce the need to run the main engine overnight, paying for themselves in less than a year.

2. Maintain Optimal Tire Pressure

Under-inflated tires increase rolling resistance, forcing the engine to work harder. Studies show that for every 10 PSI a tire is under-inflated, fuel efficiency drops by 1%. Implement a strict pre-trip inspection protocol to ensure tires are always at their manufacturer-recommended pressure.

3. Implement Predictive Routing

Stop-and-go traffic kills fuel economy. Using intelligent GPS routing systems that factor in real-time traffic, elevation changes, and weather conditions can help drivers maintain a steady cruising speed, which is the sweet spot for diesel engines.

4. Aerodynamic Upgrades

At highway speeds, over half of a truck's fuel goes toward overcoming aerodynamic drag. Installing side skirts, roof fairings, and trailer tail devices can reduce drag by up to 15%, translating directly to massive fuel savings on long-haul routes.

5. Smooth Driving Habits

Aggressive driving—rapid acceleration and hard braking—consumes significantly more fuel. Encourage drivers to anticipate traffic flow, use cruise control on flat highways, and coast to a stop when approaching red lights.
